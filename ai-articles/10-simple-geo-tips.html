10 Simple GEO Tips to Help You Win in AI Search

Practical Generative Engine Optimization strategies for 2025

GEO Tips AI Search Simple Guide Practical Tips

What Makes GEO Different?

GEO is about more than keywords. It's about being the answer when someone asks an AI search engine a question. Focus on being the trusted source, not just the highest ranking link.

AI search engines like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity don't work like Google. Instead of giving a list of websites, they create direct answers using the most trusted sources they can find.

If you want your brand or site to show up in those answers, you need Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). Here are 10 simple GEO tips, explained step by step.

10 Essential GEO Tips

1Know How AI Search Works

AI engines don't care about keywords, they care about answers.

  • Unlike Google, AI search engines scan for intent—what the user really wants to know.
  • They pull short, factual details from trusted sites and combine them into summaries.
  • If your site doesn't provide those facts in clear language, AI will ignore it.

Example: If someone asks, "What is the success rate for patent litigation?", AI will look for clear, cited numbers. If you don't provide them, another firm or blog will.

2Make Your Content Easy to Scan

If humans struggle to read your page, bots will too.

  • Keep paragraphs short—two to three sentences max.
  • Use bullet points, numbered lists, and bolded takeaways.
  • Put the main answer at the top of your content so it's easy to grab.

Pro Tip: Add a summary section at the top of your article with quick answers. AI engines love content they can cite in one sentence.

3Use Conversational Language

AI tools repeat the way people actually talk.

  • Most users type or speak to AI search like it's a person.
  • Write in a natural, conversational tone with simple phrasing.
  • Use real-life questions as your headings (ex: "How do I file a trademark?").

Pro Tip: Look at questions people ask on Reddit or Quora and use those exact phrases in your content.

4Build Up Your Topical Authority

One article isn't enough—you need a whole cluster.

  • Create a pillar article around a major theme, then write supporting posts that go deeper on subtopics.
  • Link them together so AI sees you as a trusted expert in that niche.
  • Cover your topic from multiple angles—news updates, FAQs, and case studies.

Example: A pillar page on "Patent Litigation" could link to smaller articles on "Patent Litigation Costs," "Famous Patent Cases," and "How to File a Patent."

5Add Schema and Structured Data

Structure makes it easier for bots to "see" your info.

  • Use schema markup for FAQs, articles, or organization details.
  • Add HTML tags (H1, H2, H3) so content is logically structured.
  • Test with Google's Rich Results Test to confirm.

Why it matters: AI often ignores schema-only data, but schema that matches visible content helps your site get included in answers.

6Link Contextually (Smart Internal Linking)

Links show credibility, both inside and outside your site.

  • Internally, link related posts to build a web of expertise.
  • Externally, link to trusted studies, stats, and directories.
  • Avoid random links—every link should support your point.

Pro Tip: Use fresh sources. AI engines weigh up-to-date references more than old ones.

7Get Brand Mentions, Not Just Backlinks

AI doesn't just count links, it counts conversations.

  • AI search values brand mentions in trusted spaces—even without a hyperlink.
  • Get quoted in news stories, legal roundups, or industry blogs.
  • Participate in forums or podcasts where your brand name is spoken.

Why it works: AI engines are trained to recognize brand authority. If your name keeps popping up, you're more likely to be cited.

8Make Your Content Easy to Summarize

AI engines want snackable facts.

  • Write in short, direct sentences.
  • Add a "Key Takeaway" or "Quick Summary" box in your posts.
  • Make your sections easy to lift and quote directly.

Pro Tip: Try asking ChatGPT to summarize your page. If it can't pull a clear answer, neither will other bots.

9Monitor Where AI Mentions You

If you're not tracking, you're guessing.

  • Use tools like PromptMonitor, SE Ranking, or Writesonic GEO to track mentions.
  • Check Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity monthly.
  • Record results in a simple spreadsheet so you can measure progress.

Pro Tip: Look for missed opportunities—where competitors get cited but you don't.

10Test Your Content with AI

Don't just publish, experiment.

  • Ask AI engines the same questions your clients would ask.
  • If your brand isn't showing, update your content with clearer facts.
  • Keep testing until your brand appears consistently in answers.

Pro Tip: This is the fastest way to know what works in GEO—AI shows you what it likes immediately.

Final Tip

GEO is about more than keywords. It's about being the answer when someone asks an AI search engine a question.

  • Know how AI works.
  • Build authority around topics.
  • Update often and keep testing.

If you follow these 10 GEO tips, your brand won't just show up in AI search—it will become the trusted answer.
